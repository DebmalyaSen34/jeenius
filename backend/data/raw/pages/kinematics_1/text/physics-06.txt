Freely Falling Bodies

A common example of motion in a straight line
with constant acceleration is free fall of a body near
the earth’s surface. If air resistance is neglected and
a body is dropped near the surface of the earth, it falls
along a vertical straight line. The acceleration is in the
vertically downward direction and its magnitude is
almost constant if the height is small as compared with
the radius of the earth (6400 km). This magnitude is
approximately equal to 9°8 m/s” or 32 ft/s” and is
denoted by the letter g.
