3.5 AVERAGE ACCELERATION AND
INSTANTANEOUS ACCELERATION

If the velocity of a particle remains constant as
time passes, we say that it is moving with uniform
velocity. If the velocity changes with time, it is said to
be accelerated. The acceleration is the rate of change
of velocity. Velocity is a vector quantity hence a change
in its magnitude or direction or both will change the
velocity.
